Frequently Asked Questions

Your premium is affected by several factors including the location of your home, its age and construction type, the coverage limits you choose, your deductible amount, your claims history, and security features in your home. Michigan-specific risks like wind, hail, and winter weather are also considered.

Standard home insurance policies typically do not cover flood damage in Michigan. You'll need to purchase a separate flood insurance policy, especially if you live in a flood-prone area. We can help you assess your flood risk and find the right coverage for your needs.

You can lower your premiums by increasing your deductible, installing safety features like security systems or smoke detectors, bundling your home and auto insurance, maintaining a good credit score, and shopping around for the best rates. We can help you find all available discounts.

Actual cash value coverage pays for the replacement cost minus depreciation, while replacement cost coverage pays the full cost to replace damaged items without deducting for depreciation. We generally recommend replacement cost coverage as it provides more comprehensive protection.

The claims process timeline varies depending on the complexity of the claim. For straightforward claims, you can expect an adjuster visit within 24-48 hours, and payment within a week to 10 days. We'll guide you through every step and help expedite the process.

What We Cover

Dwelling Coverage

Protection for your home's structure against damage from fire, wind, hail, and other covered perils specific to Michigan.

Personal Property

Covers your furniture, electronics, clothing, and other personal belongings against theft or damage, even when away from home.

Liability Protection

Protects you if someone is injured on your property or if you cause damage to someone else's property, covering legal fees and medical expenses.

Other Structures

Covers structures on your property that are not attached to your home, such as garages, sheds, fences, and guesthouses.

Loss of Use

Provides coverage for additional living expenses if you're unable to live in your home due to a covered loss, including hotel costs and meals.

Michigan-Specific Risks

Additional coverage for Michigan-specific risks such as winter weather damage, wind storms, and seasonal flooding concerns.
