Michigan Home Insurance FAQs

Answers to common questions about home insurance in Michigan.

Standard policies often exclude flood damage (including Great Lakes shoreline flooding), earthquakes, sewer backups, and intentional damage. Many also have limits on high-value items like jewelry. We offer endorsements to cover many of these gaps.

Insurers consider your home's location (including proximity to lakes and flood zones), age, construction type, claims history, coverage amounts, and deductibles. Michigan's weather risks make location particularly important here.

Unlike auto insurance, Michigan doesn't legally require homeowners insurance. However, if you have a mortgage, your lender will require it. Even without a mortgage, going without coverage is extremely risky given Michigan's weather.

Consider bundling policies, increasing deductibles, installing protective devices (like storm shutters), maintaining good credit, and asking about discounts for claims-free history, new roofs, or retired homeowners. We'll help identify all available savings.
